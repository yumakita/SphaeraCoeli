Positions: Keplerian with Meeus lunar correction (~0.3° Moon accuracy)

Planetary positions computed from J2000 Keplerian elements — approximate, for contemplative use.

Geocentric, as per Western tropical astrology. Kepler (1571–1630) proved orbits are ellipses, not circles, from Tycho Brahe's naked-eye observations — a break from two millennia of circular astronomy. J2000 elements are accurate to within ~1° for outer planets over short spans, but degrade significantly beyond ±200 years from the present. The Moon is least accurate throughout. For precision work, use the Swiss Ephemeris.
